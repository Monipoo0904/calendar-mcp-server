THE ACADEMY — CURRICULUM GUIDE
3rd Grade ELA Skills & Benchmarks
Source: Curriculum Guide, Section 3

==============================================================
SECTION 1 — READING FOUNDATIONS
==============================================================

By the end of 3rd grade, students are expected to read grade-level texts
fluently and with purpose. Core reading skills at this level include:

PHONICS & WORD RECOGNITION
- Decode multisyllabic words using knowledge of prefixes, suffixes, and root words
- Use context clues to determine the meaning of unfamiliar vocabulary
- Recognize and correctly read irregularly spelled grade-level words
- Distinguish long and short vowel patterns in one- and two-syllable words

READING FLUENCY
- Read grade-level prose and poetry orally with accuracy, appropriate rate, and expression
- Target oral reading rate: 90–110 words per minute with high accuracy
- Use self-correction strategies when comprehension breaks down
- Reread sentences to improve understanding of complex passages

READING COMPREHENSION — LITERARY TEXT
- Ask and answer questions to demonstrate understanding of a text
- Recount stories, including fables and folk tales from diverse cultures
- Describe characters in a story and explain how their actions contribute to the plot
- Determine the central message, lesson, or moral of a story
- Compare and contrast the themes, settings, and plots of stories by the same author

READING COMPREHENSION — INFORMATIONAL TEXT
- Determine the main idea of a text and explain how key details support it
- Describe the relationship between a series of historical events or scientific concepts
- Determine the meaning of domain-specific words and phrases in a text
- Describe the logical connection between sentences and paragraphs
- Compare and contrast the most important points in two texts on the same topic

==============================================================
SECTION 2 — WRITING SKILLS
==============================================================

OPINION WRITING
- Introduce the topic, state an opinion, and create an organizational structure
- Provide reasons that support the opinion using linking words (because, therefore, since)
- Provide a concluding statement or section

INFORMATIVE / EXPLANATORY WRITING
- Introduce a topic, use facts and definitions, and provide a concluding statement
- Use linking words to connect ideas (also, another, and, more, but)
- Develop a topic with facts, definitions, and details

NARRATIVE WRITING
- Establish a situation and introduce a narrator and/or characters
- Use temporal words (first, then, next, finally) to signal event order
- Provide a sense of closure at the end of the narrative

==============================================================
SECTION 3 — LANGUAGE SKILLS
==============================================================

GRAMMAR
- Explain the function of nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, and adverbs
- Form and use regular and irregular plural nouns
- Use abstract nouns correctly in sentences
- Form and use the simple verb tenses (past, present, future)
- Ensure subject–verb and pronoun–antecedent agreement

VOCABULARY
- Determine or clarify the meaning of unknown words using context clues
- Identify real-life connections between words and their use
- Distinguish shades of meaning among related words
- Use glossaries and dictionaries (print or digital) to determine meanings

==============================================================
SECTION 4 — BENCHMARK ASSESSMENTS
==============================================================

Mid-cohort check (Week 5):
- Running record at grade level text (accuracy ≥ 95%)
- Retelling rubric for literary text (3-point scale)
- Written response to informational passage (paragraph form)

End-of-cohort assessment (Week 10):
- Fluency passage (90-second timed read, score words correct per minute)
- Constructed response to two-part question
- Opinion writing sample scored against 4-point rubric

Coaches review assessment data in the skills database and update
the student record after each assessment cycle.
